My opinion on Windows 11

The UI

The UI itself is decent, granted I've used Windows my entire life, so I'm kind of used to it, from XP to 11. UI seems like it could be easily recreated through KDE Plasma or Cinnamon. Not much for me to comment on here, aside from the fact that I hate that the default design for Windows 11 is to put the start menu in the center, considering I came from Windows 10 at the time.

The Software

Well, I don't really use the Microsoft Office Suite aside from School, where I get it for free, though I do use Outlook, though I also use Gmail as well. I either use Google Suite or Libreoffice anyways. I am also tired of the attempt at shoving AI into where it doesn't need to be, like Paint, and Notepad. The majority of my software (Aside from the FRC Driver Station by NI), is either open source or multiplatform.

Gaming

Well, to be fair, it is the gold standard for gaming, due to the fact that it holds over 60% of the global market share, and is the majority on Steam. However, as Valve released Proton, a compatibility layer allowing Windows Games to be played on Linux, gaming on Linux is an option.
Despite Proton being on Linux and not macOS (Because of Apple and their insistence on proprietary software), this should allow me to play most of my games I love to play, primarily due to my favorites either being multiplatform or console exclusive. So gaming can be handled better elsewhere.

The Verdict

Once I can afford an NVME for my Mini PC, I'll switch to Linux and transfer files I care about onto that drive, and then wipe the Windows drive to use as extra storage.

TLDR: I plan on switching from Windows
